Model Overview

This chess win/draw/loss predictor uses logistic regression models trained on data from Lichess games. The models predict the probability of winning, drawing, or losing a game based on the current Stockfish evaluation and the average Elo rating of the players.

Lichess uses a similar logistic regression model trained only on high-level games to classify moves as inaccuracies, mistakes, or blunders in game analyses.

Data Source

The training data comes from publicly available Lichess games from December 2025. Each game includes move-by-move Stockfish evaluations and player Elo ratings.

Model Training

Games are grouped by the average Elo rating of the players into ranges such as 800-1000, 1000-1200, etc. Separate logistic regression models are trained for each Elo range to account for skill differences; since lower-rated players are more likely to make mistakes, the chances of a player losing from a winning position or winning from a losing position are higher.

While games were grouped into bins based on the average Elo rating of the two players, it is generally sufficient for a player to enter their own Elo rating in the calculator. Lichess and other chess platforms attempt to match players with similar rankings for online games.

The model uses the Stockfish evaluation as the primary feature. Evaluations are measured in pawns (e.g., +1.5 means White is up by 1.5 pawns).

Positions with forced checkmate sequences and forced stalemate sequences were excluded from the model's training data.

Limitations

  • The model does not handle forced mate sequences (evaluations like #5).
  • Predictions are based on historical data and may not reflect real-time play.
  • The model assumes standard chess rules and does not account for special situations like time pressure.
  • The model does not account for how "sharp" a position is; in other words, an evaluation that relies on a player finding one specific move is viewed the same as an evaluation with several good move options.
  • The model only includes games where a player chose to request a Stockfish evaluation from the Lichess interface. This may result in a biased selection of games, such as games where a player has an advantageous position, blunders, and loses or draws.

Technical Details

The models are multinomial logistic regression classifiers implemented using scikit-learn. They predict three classes: loss (0), draw (1), or win (2) for the player to move.
